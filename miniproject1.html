I am a 2nd year student here at CCSF. I am originally from New York City and moved to San Francisco months before COVID. It is around this time that I decided to go back to school and pursue a career in cybersecurity like I always wanted to. I started out with a few Udemy courses on the basics of networking, computer hardware and computer software. I worked my way up from there and watched a few tutorials on Youtube and a bunch of other websites and forums.

So far I am learning a lot in the training field of cybersecurity. Thanks to websites like Udemy and Tryhackme, I have plenty of resources at my disposal. TryHackMe is a website that holds the beginners hand in the beginning stages of learning basics of Linux/Hacking/Networking/Computer hardware, etc. Udemy is a website where creators and software developers/engineers can go and sell their courses and there are also professionals from the fields of these subjects and they teach. Some of these courses can run up to hundreds of dollars but if you catch them on a good sale day, you can luck out and purchase a course for $11.99.

I also take advantage of CCSFs Free City program to get the proper structure when learning these new subjects. So far, my education has led me to build 10 computers (and counting) along with setting up my own personal home lab. Next semester I will be diving into the ethical hacking part of cybersecurity and am stoked about it.
